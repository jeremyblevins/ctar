Cyber Threat Assessment and Reporting (CTAR) Framework

Making Cyber Threat Intelligence Understandable

An open educational framework that helps students, educators, researchers, and cybersecurity practitioners understand cyber threat intelligence through structured, evidence-based analysis.

Vision

To make cyber threat intelligence accessible, transparent, and teachable for everyone.

Why CTAR?

Cybersecurity is filled with headlines, technical reports, vendor research, government advisories, and social media commentary. While these sources often provide valuable information, they rarely explain how conclusions were reached, how reliable the evidence is, or what uncertainty remains.

CTAR was created to help bridge that gap by teaching structured, evidence-based analytical thinking.

  • What evidence supports this claim?
  • Where did this information originate?
  • How reliable are the available sources?
  • What assumptions are being made?
  • What alternative explanations exist?
  • How confident should we be?

Use Cases

Learn from Cybersecurity News

Start with a trusted article and build a deeper understanding through additional research, source evaluation, technical context, and structured analysis.

Research Historical Incidents

Investigate cyber events such as ILOVEYOU, Stuxnet, WannaCry, SolarWinds, and NotPetya using publicly available evidence.

Conduct Independent Research

Build structured assessments from vulnerabilities, malware families, threat actors, or ongoing campaigns.

Classroom Learning

Support inquiry-based learning by helping students evaluate evidence, compare sources, and communicate analytical conclusions.

Professional Research

Organize evidence, document analytical reasoning, and produce transparent cyber threat assessments.

Guiding Principles

Evidence First

Support conclusions with verifiable evidence.

Transparency

Clearly distinguish facts, assumptions, analysis, confidence, and uncertainty.

Critical Thinking

Encourage questioning and corroboration.

Education

Teach analytical reasoning rather than memorization.

AI Independence

Designed to work with any AI platform—or none at all.

Open Knowledge

Centered on shared knowledge and community contribution.

The CTAR Project

The CTAR Project is an open educational initiative dedicated to improving cybersecurity education through transparent, evidence-based analytical practices.

The project welcomes contributions from educators, students, researchers, cybersecurity practitioners, technical writers, and software developers.

AI Collaboration

The CTAR Framework was developed through an iterative collaboration between its founder and generative artificial intelligence. Human judgment guided the educational philosophy, methodology, and final decisions, while AI served as a collaborative partner for brainstorming, drafting, editing, and exploring alternatives.

What CTAR Is Not

  • Not a commercial threat intelligence platform
  • Not an attribution authority
  • Not a replacement for professional intelligence tradecraft
  • Not an autonomous AI analyst
  • Not a cybersecurity product

CTAR is an educational framework that teaches structured, evidence-based cyber threat analysis.
